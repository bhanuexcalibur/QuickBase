Product Launch Playbook

From Prompt to Production

Launch strategy for Quickbase Pave, announced April 28, 2026. How to position and scale the first AI app builder that moves teams from prototype to governed, production-ready applications.

Positioning by Buyer

One product. Three buyers. Three different reasons to care.

Operations Leaders
Problem: Workflow requests sit in a backlog for 6 months because engineering is blocked.

Pave Positioning: Ship custom apps in weeks, not quarters. Your team owns the app from day one.
IT / Platform Leadership
Problem: You want to empower ops teams to build, but need visibility and control. Most AI builders have zero governance.

Pave Positioning: Governance by design. IT maintains oversight. Permissions, SSO, audit logs, data residency built in.
Finance / Procurement
Problem: Other AI platforms have hidden costs that scale unpredictably as usage grows.

Pave Positioning: Transparent, predictable pricing. You know exactly what you're paying before you scale.

Launch Messaging

Position the launch around one message: Pave is built for production, not prototypes.

Primary Headline
Pave: From Prompt to Production. The first AI app builder built for scale, not prototypes.
Supporting Narrative
Most AI app builders focus on speed: get from idea to code fast. Pave focuses on completion: get from idea to production fast. That means built-in governance so IT stays in control. Predictable pricing so finance knows the cost upfront. Secure infrastructure so data stays where it should. Pave is the path from experimentation to execution.
Output Prototypes, POCs Production-ready apps Governance Minimal to none Built-in Infrastructure Vendor patchwork Included Pricing Hidden, unpredictable costs Transparent

Sales Enablement

Sales teams close faster when they talk about problems, not features.

30-Second Pitches by Buyer

For COO / Operations
"Your team describes a workflow problem in plain language. Pave builds the app. It's live by end of week. No engineering queue. No waiting."
For CTO / IT
"We can let ops teams build without giving up control. Pave has governance built in: permissions, SSO, audit logs. IT knows exactly what's running."
For CFO / Finance
"Pave lets ops teams build their own apps without engineer overhead. Transparent pricing, no surprise costs. You know what you're paying upfront."

Common Objection Handlers

vs. ChatGPT / Claude
"Those tools are great for ideation, but they don't cover the last mile. You still need to deploy, secure, scale, and maintain the app. Pave includes all of that."
vs. Low-Code Platforms
"Low-code is for builders who know what they're doing. Pave is built for ops teams who know their problem but aren't developers. Zero learning curve."
